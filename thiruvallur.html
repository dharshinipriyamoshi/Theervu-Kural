History and Geography

Thiruvallur is a historically significant district in Tamil Nadu, known for its ancient temples and cultural heritage. The region was once ruled by the Pallavas, Cholas, and Vijayanagara kings. It is located near Chennai and has a mix of urban and rural areas. The district experiences a tropical climate with hot summers and moderate rainfall. Agriculture, trade, and industries play a crucial role in the district’s economy. The presence of reservoirs and rivers like the Cooum and Kusasthalai makes it an important region for water resources.

Tourism

Thiruvallur is famous for its temples and natural attractions. The Veera Raghava Perumal Temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, is an important pilgrimage site. Poondi Reservoir, a major water source for Chennai, is a scenic picnic spot. Pulicat Lake, the second-largest brackish water lake in India, is home to migratory birds and a paradise for bird watchers. The Thiruthani Murugan Temple, one of the six abodes of Lord Murugan, attracts thousands of devotees. The district also has several parks and lakes, making it a pleasant place for tourists.
